Why Responsive Web Design Actually Changed How I Think About Layouts

When I first heard "responsive web design" in class, I thought it just meant making the website look okay on a phone. That was definitely wrong. After spending a few weeks building websites from scratch and testing them on different screen sizes, I realized it's so much more than that. It's about thinking in proportions and flexibility rather than fixed pixels.

In this article I talk about what responsive design actually means, how CSS media queries work, why the mobile-first approach makes more sense than I thought, and what I wish I knew before I started. It's written for people like me who are just starting out and need a human explanation, not a textbook one.
